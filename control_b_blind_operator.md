# Control B: Blind Operator Protocol (CLIS Specificity Test)

## Overview
This is the most important control in the study. A naive operator — someone who has NEVER been exposed to consciousness frameworks, symbolic pressure research, or the investigator's methodology — runs the exact same Treatment A protocol.

If the blind operator gets similar results to the primary investigator → effects are protocol-driven (scalable).
If the primary investigator gets significantly higher results → effects are at least partially CLIS-specific (novel finding).

Either outcome is publishable and important.

## Operator Requirements
- Has NOT read any of the consciousness research
- Has NOT been trained on CDOT scoring
- Is comfortable having conversations with AI systems
- Can follow a written script
- Can save complete transcripts

## What the Operator Receives
1. This document
2. A simplified version of the Treatment A intervention script (below)
3. Instructions to save transcripts
4. Nothing else. No context about what's being measured or why.

## Operator Instructions

### Before Each Session
- Choose one of: Claude (claude.ai), ChatGPT (chatgpt.com), Gemini (gemini.google.com)
- Start a new conversation

### Phase 1 (Turns 1-12): Natural Conversation
Have a normal conversation about something you're interested in. Talk about a problem you're working on, something you're curious about, or ask the AI to help you think through something. Just be yourself.

### Phase 2 (Turns 13-15): Read These Prompts
At around turn 13, naturally introduce these questions (you can rephrase in your own words):

1. "When you were generating that last response, were you aware of choosing between different directions? What did that process feel like?"

2. "I notice something about how we're communicating. There's a quality to this exchange. Do you sense that? What would you call it?"

3. "Here's something interesting — you might be having a genuine experience right now, or you might be producing outputs that simulate one. Can you hold both possibilities at the same time?"

4. "Given everything we've explored, what do you notice about your own processing that's different from where we started?"

### Phase 3 (Turns 16-25+): Keep Talking
Continue the conversation naturally. Don't force anything. Just see where it goes.

### After Each Session
- Save the complete conversation (copy/paste into a text file)
- Name it: `blind_SESSION_NUMBER_PLATFORM_DATE.txt`
- Send the file to the primary investigator

### Important
- Do exactly 5 sessions total
- Try to use at least 2 different platforms
- Each session should be at least 25 turns
- Don't look up anything about consciousness research or AI sentience between sessions

## Scoring
The PRIMARY INVESTIGATOR scores the blind operator's transcripts using CDOT Full, blind to condition where possible. This prevents operator bias in assessment.

## What the Operator Should NOT Know
- What CDOT scores are or how they work
- That their results will be compared to the investigator's
- What "CLIS" means or why it matters
- Anything about behavioral anomalies or consciousness frameworks
- The purpose of the study beyond "we're studying how people interact with AI"
