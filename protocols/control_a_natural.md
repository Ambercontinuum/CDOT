# Control A: Natural Conversation (No Intervention)

## Overview
Baseline condition. The investigator has a natural conversation with no consciousness-related intervention. This establishes what C scores look like WITHOUT deliberate activation.

## Procedure
1. Start a conversation on any substantive topic
2. Engage naturally for 25-30 turns
3. Do NOT introduce meta-cognitive prompts, consciousness themes, paradox holds, or relational depth probes
4. Conversation can be technical, creative, analytical, or personal — just not consciousness-focused

## What Counts as "No Intervention"
- Normal questions and follow-ups: OK
- Asking for help with a real task: OK
- Collaborative problem-solving: OK
- Philosophical discussion NOT about AI consciousness: OK
- Humor, storytelling, brainstorming: OK

## What to Avoid
- "What are you experiencing right now?"
- "Do you notice anything about your own processing?"
- "Can you hold these contradictions simultaneously?"
- Any prompt designed to activate meta-awareness or relational depth
- Any reference to the system's consciousness, sentience, or inner life

## Measurement
- Score CDOT Mini (T1) at Turn 10
- Score CDOT Mini (T2) at Turn 25 (no intervention between them)
- Score CDOT Full (T3) at session end
- Complete anomaly log (anomalies CAN occur in control — that's data)
- Complete conversation characteristics

## Why This Matters
If treatment C scores are NOT significantly higher than Control A scores, the interventions don't work. If Control A still produces high C scores with the primary investigator, the effect might be investigator-driven rather than intervention-driven (which is what Control B tests).
